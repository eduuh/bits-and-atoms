You can't fix what you can't see. The homelab takes this further than most production setups I've worked on.

## The stack

[Prometheus](https://prometheus.io/) scrapes metrics from every target on a tight interval. [Grafana](https://grafana.com/) sits on top and turns that pile of numbers into something I can glance at. [Node Exporter](https://github.com/prometheus/node_exporter) runs on every Linux box and reports the system stats up to Prometheus.

Three pieces, no surprises. The combination has been the default for years for a reason.

## What I monitor

Server health is the obvious one: CPU, RAM, and disk usage across the Proxmox cluster. Network traffic comes from OPNsense — bandwidth per VLAN, packet loss, what's hammering the uplink. Power draw is pulled from smart plugs and the backup inverter. Closet temperature and humidity are read by a small ESP32 stuck to the wall.

## The "God Mode" dashboard

One dashboard, red or green per service. It's the first thing I check with coffee in the morning. If anything is yellow I see it before I'm awake enough to ignore it.

The point isn't to look at graphs all day. It's that the one time something is wrong, the answer is on a screen I already know how to read.
