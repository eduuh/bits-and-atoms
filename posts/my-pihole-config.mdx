Every device on my network routes DNS through [Pi-hole](https://pi-hole.net/). No browser extensions, no per-device tweaks. Block once, block everywhere.

## The setup

Pi-hole runs as a container in my home lab. A Raspberry Pi works just as well — the workload is tiny and the memory footprint barely registers.

## Recursive DNS with Unbound

I don't forward queries to Google (8.8.8.8) or Cloudflare (1.1.1.1). Pi-hole hands them to [Unbound](https://nlnetlabs.nl/projects/unbound/about/), which walks the DNS tree itself: ask the root, then the TLD, then the authoritative server.

Two things fall out of that. No single company sees my DNS history. And DNSSEC validation happens locally rather than on someone else's resolver.

## Regex blocking

Blocklists cover the easy stuff. Regex covers the rest. One pattern catches a whole subdomain of mobile-ad telemetry that would otherwise need fifty individual entries.

## Local DNS

Pi-hole is also my local DNS server. I type `plex.home.arpa` instead of remembering `192.168.30.42`. The home network gets the convenience of a real domain without anything leaving the LAN.
